Multi-year Deal Rules
Year 1 — At Close
AE earns commission on ACV (Year 1 ARR) at the point of closing the deal.
Year 2+ — Upon Renewal
AE earns again on each renewal (Year 2, Year 3, etc.) at the same rate. Renewal commission is banked and visible in advance.
Banked View
AEs can see commission already provisionally earned for future years. Motivational — "I have $X already banked for next year."

Classification Logic
Source vs Type
Understanding the two fields and how they determine commission rate
HubSpot field
Source
Where the lead originally came from. Set by the AE in HubSpot when creating the deal.
Possible values
Outbound: LinkedInAE-sourced via LinkedIn
Outbound: SDR'sSDR-sourced, handed to AE
Outbound: GTMGTM campaign-sourced
Outbound: PaidPaid ads, AE-led outbound
Inbound: PaidMarketing paid ad, came inbound
Organic⚠ Ambiguous — see note below
ReferralAlways Inbound 5% regardless
Upsell CampaignExisting customer expansion
Organic is ambiguous. A deal marked as "Organic" could be an AE who cold-prospected a customer who had never engaged with WorkflowMAX — that's Outbound. Or it could be a customer who found the product themselves. Without Commission Attribution set, the model defaults to Inbound 5%.
Calculated by artefact
Type
The commission classification. Computed by the artefact from Source + Commission Attribution. Determines the rate paid to the AE.
3 possible values
Outbound 10%
AE actively hunted this deal. Source contains "Outbound" OR AE had Heavy Involvement / Sourced on an inbound/organic lead — picking it up early stage through close.
Upsell 7.5%
Expansion of an existing customer. Deal ID is in the confirmed upsell list OR source = "Upsell Campaign".
Inbound 5%
Lead came through an external channel AND the AE did not have Heavy Involvement. Source = Referral always = 5% regardless of involvement. Non Sourced = 5%.
How Type is calculated — decision order
1
Upsell?
Deal ID in confirmed upsell list or Source = "Upsell Campaign" → Upsell 7.5%. Absolute priority.
2
Source contains "Outbound"?
→ Outbound 10%. Explicit outbound channel — no conflict possible.
3
Source = "Referral"?
→ Inbound 5% always. Referral overrides involvement — WorkflowMAX is already on the hook for a commercial payout.
4
AE Involvement = Heavy Involvement or Sourced?
→ Outbound 10%. This is the key override. Even if Source = Organic or Inbound, if the AE picked it up early and drove it to close with Heavy Involvement, they earn 10%. Ryan reviews line by line each quarter.
5
Source = Inbound or Organic, no strong involvement?
→ Inbound 5%. Lead came through an external channel and the AE didn't claim heavy involvement.
6
AE Involvement = Non Sourced / no source set?
Non Sourced → Inbound 5%. No source and no involvement → Outbound 10% (default — AE created the record).
AE Involvement wins over Source when there is a conflict
If a lead came in via an organic or inbound channel but the AE picked it up at early stage and drove it to close with Heavy Involvement, the AE is entitled to full commission (10%). This is intentional — the AE's work is what converted the deal, not the channel. The exception is Referral, which always stays at 5% because WorkflowMAX is already paying a commercial referral fee. Ryan reviews every Organic + Heavy Involvement deal line by line each quarter to validate the attribution.
